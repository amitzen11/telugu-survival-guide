# 10. Household Management & Groceries

Effective household management relies on clear instructions to domestic support staff and the ability to navigate local `Rythu Bazars` (Farmers' Markets) or `Kirana` (grocery) stores. The staff often predominantly speak Telugu, and local vendors might not know English terminology for spices and vegetables.

## 1. Interacting with Domestic Help (Maids & Nannies)

Set clear boundaries with respectful (but direct) instructions. **Do not use the bare verb roots!** Always utilize the respectful `-andi` suffix since these individuals manage your home.

| Task Category | Telugu Instruction | English Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cleaning** | **Illu chakkaga choodandi** ⟨Il-lu chak-ka-ga choo-dan-di⟩ | Please keep the house clean. |
| **Hygiene** | **Chethulu kadugandi** ⟨Chay-thu-lu ka-du-gan-di⟩ | Wash your hands. |
| **Errands** | **Paalu teeyandi** ⟨Paa-lu tee-yan-di⟩ | Bring the milk. |
| **Verification** | **Pani poorthi chesara?** ⟨Pa-ni poor-thi chay-sa-ra?⟩ | Have you finished the work? |
| **Scheduling** | **Repu meeru raavacha?** ⟨Ray-pu mee-ru raa-va-cha?⟩ | Can you come tomorrow? |
| **Payments** | **Dabbulu teesukondi** ⟨Dab-bu-lu tee-su-kon-di⟩ | Take your payment. |
| **Feedback** | **Pani baagundi** ⟨Pa-ni baa-gun-di⟩ | The work is good. |
| **Warning** | **Jagratha-ga pani cheyyandi** ⟨Ja-gra-tha-ga pa-ni chey-yan-di⟩ | Work carefully. |

## 2. Grocery Shopping Lexicon

When you hit the market, you will need the local vocabulary for vegetables and dry goods.

### The Vegetable Market

| English | Telugu | Hindi Bridge |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Potato** | **Bangala dumpa / Alugadda** ⟨A-lu-gad-da⟩ | Aaloo |
| **Onion** | **Ullipaaya / Yerragadda** ⟨Ul-li-paa-ya⟩ | Pyaaz |
| **Tomato** | **Tamota** ⟨Ta-mo-ta⟩ | Tamatar |
| **Ladies Finger** | **Bendakaya** ⟨Ben-da-ka-ya⟩ | Bhindi |
| **Brinjal** | **Vankaya** ⟨Van-ka-ya⟩ | Baingan |
| **Green Chilli** | **Pachimirapa** ⟨Pach-ch-mi-ra-pa⟩ | Hari Mirch |
| **Garlic** | **Vellulli** ⟨Vel-lul-li⟩ | Lehsun |
| **Ginger** | **Allam** ⟨Al-lam⟩ | Adrak |
| **Lemon** | **Nimmakaya** ⟨Nim-ma-ka-ya⟩ | Nimbu |
| **Coconut** | **Kobbari** ⟨Kob-ba-ri⟩ | Nariyal |

### Spices and Kitchen Staples

| English | Telugu | Hindi Bridge |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Turmeric** | **Pasupu** ⟨Pa-su-pu⟩ | Haldi |
| **Mustard** | **Avalu** ⟨Aa-va-lu⟩ | Sarson |
| **Cumin** | **Jilakara** ⟨Ji-la-ka-ra⟩ | Jeera |
| **Salt** | **Uppu** ⟨Up-pu⟩ | Namak |
| **Tamarind** | **Chintapandu** ⟨Chin-ta-pan-du⟩ | Imli |
| **Pot/Vessel** | **Ginne** ⟨Gin-ne⟩ | Katori / Bartan |

> **Cultural Note:** Don't stress if you forget a word! Hyderabad's Rythu Bazars are chaotic and forgiving. Pointing, combined with "Idi kaavali" (I want this) works wonders.
